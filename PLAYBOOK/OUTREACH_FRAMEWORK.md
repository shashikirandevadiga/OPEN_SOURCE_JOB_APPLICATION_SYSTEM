## ðŸ”„ **AAKASH GUPTA'S 3 C'S FRAMEWORK**

**Every message must be:**

### **1. CUSTOMIZED**
- Research the specific person and their role
- Reference their recent work, posts, or achievements
- Mention specific details about the company/product
- No templates - each message uniquely crafted

### **2. COMPELLING** 
- Provide value or genuine interest in their work
- Include relevant achievement that demonstrates capability
- Show how you can contribute to their specific challenges
- Avoid sounding desperate or generic

### **3. CONCISE**
- Keep messages short and scannable
- Get to the point quickly
- Include specific ask with easy next step
- Respect their time with brevity

### **4. MESSAGE LENGTH GUIDELINES**

**Character Count Targets:**
- **Initial outreach (Tier 1):** 600-800 characters (concise, scannable)
- **First escalation (Tier 2):** 400-600 characters (shorter, value-focused)
- **Final follow-up (Tier 3):** 300-500 characters (brief, respectful)

**Why These Lengths?**
- **600-800**: Enough context to demonstrate research without overwhelming
- **400-600**: Reminder of value prop without repeating initial message
- **300-500**: Respectful final touch, acknowledges they may not be interested

**Verification**: Use `echo "[message]" | wc -c` to verify character count before sending.

## ðŸ”„ **ENHANCED ESCALATION FRAMEWORK**

**Every track uses a 3-tier escalation system based on Aakash Gupta's methodology:**

### **TIER 1: SOFT ASK (Initial Contact)**
**When to Use:** First message to anyone  
**Ask Level:** General referral mention, low commitment  

**Language Options:**
- "I would appreciate any referral support you might offer"
- "Would love your guidance on the hiring process"
- "Happy for any insights about the opportunity"

### **TIER 2: MEDIUM ASK (If They Respond Positively)**
**When to Use:** They reply asking for more info or seem engaged  
**Ask Level:** Specific action but manageable commitment  

**Language Options:**
- "Would you be comfortable forwarding my resume to the recruiting team?"
- "Could you mention to recruiting that you know someone interested?"
- "Would you mind sharing my background with the hiring team?"

### **TIER 3: DIRECT ASK (If They're Very Engaged)**
**When to Use:** They ask detailed questions or offer significant help  
**Ask Level:** Asking for advocacy/word with hiring manager  

**Language Options:**
- "Would you be comfortable putting in a word with the hiring manager?"
- "Could you recommend me to the PM hiring team?"
- "Would you be open to advocating for my background with leadership?"
